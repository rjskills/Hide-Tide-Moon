Your Eyes

By Rose Mary Johnson, Poetry Volume One, U.S. Copyright

The crowd was so thick.
It was almost night.
The air was cool.
But, I was not.

Like a flash in the dark, your eyes did appear.
Taunting me.
Taunting me.
Then, they would disappear.

I searched all night for your eyes.
For they took my heart to my surprise.
And, when I found them.
They left me dismayed.

For I realized I could not live without them.
Your eyes.
Your eyes.
